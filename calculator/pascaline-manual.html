1. Historical Background

In 1642, the 19-year-old French mathematician and philosopher Blaise Pascal invented the Pascaline. His goal was to help his father, a tax commissioner in Rouen, with tedious and endless arithmetic calculations. It is considered the first mechanical calculator capable of performing carries automatically.

2. How it Works

The Pascaline operated using a series of linked dials (similar to old rotary phones). Each wheel had 10 teeth representing digits 0 through 9. When a wheel moved from 9 to 0, a clever mechanism called the "sautoir" engaged to advance the adjacent wheel by one position.

3. Significance

Although not a huge commercial success (it was too expensive and delicate), the Pascaline laid the foundation for modern computing. It proved that machines could perform intellectual tasks.
